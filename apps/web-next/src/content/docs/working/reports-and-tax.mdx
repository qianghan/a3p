## Reports & tax estimates

Find these under **Tax & Reports**. Each report can be viewed on **cash or accrual** basis with a toggle, regardless of your default.

### Profit & Loss

Revenue minus expenses over any period. On cash basis, revenue is money received and expenses are cash that actually left; on accrual, it's invoiced revenue and incurred costs.

### Balance sheet

Assets, liabilities, and equity as of a date. Under accrual, unpaid customer invoices show as **Accounts Receivable** and open bills show as **Accounts Payable**, so the sheet reflects what you're owed and what you owe.

### Tax estimate

A combined estimate of income tax + self-employment/national contributions for your jurisdiction, including any W-2 / employment income you enter. Ask *"how much should I set aside for taxes?"* any time.

> **Estimates, not filings.** Every tax and payroll figure in AgentBook is an estimate to help you plan and set money aside. Confirm anything before you file, and consult a professional for advice specific to your situation.

### Hand-off to your accountant

Prior-year returns you upload are parsed to ground this year's baseline, and your accountant can review the same reports through their portal.
