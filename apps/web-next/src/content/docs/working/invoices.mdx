## Invoices & getting paid

### Create an invoice

Go to **Invoicing → New** (or ask the agent to *"invoice Acme $2,000 for March consulting"*). Add line items, a due date, and — if you bill in another currency — the agent records both the quoted and your booking amounts.

Each invoice posts to your ledger automatically, so revenue and receivables stay correct without extra steps.

### Track status

Invoices move through **draft → sent → paid** (and flag **overdue** past the due date). The dashboard shows what's outstanding at a glance.

### Get paid & auto-reconcile

When a matching deposit lands in a connected bank account, the agent marks the invoice **paid** and records the payment for you — no manual matching. Anything ambiguous is flagged for review instead of guessed.

### Retainers & subscriptions

Billing a retainer up front? AgentBook can recognize that revenue over the period (deferred revenue) so your monthly reports show earned vs unearned correctly.
