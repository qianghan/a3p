## Record your first expense

There are three ways to get an expense into AgentBook. Use whichever fits the moment.

### Snap a receipt

On the **Capture** screen (or the mobile app), take a photo of a receipt. The agent reads the vendor, amount, date, and tax with OCR, suggests a category, and asks you to confirm. Blurry photo? It'll tell you what it couldn't read.

### Tell the agent

In chat or Telegram, just say what happened:

> log $42 lunch with a client

The agent creates the expense, picks a category, and confirms. Correct it in the same breath — *"no, that's Travel"* — and it learns for next time.

### Import a spreadsheet

Have history in a CSV? Go to **Expenses → Import** and upload it. AgentBook maps the columns (date, amount, description, vendor) and auto-categorizes each row. You can review before anything is finalized.

### Reviewing and fixing

Everything the agent is unsure about goes to your **review queue** — nothing is silently guessed. Changing a category once teaches the agent your preference for that vendor going forward.
