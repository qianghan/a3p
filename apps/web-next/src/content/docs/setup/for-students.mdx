## AgentBook for students

If you're in college or university — full-time, part-time, US, Canada, or Australia — AgentBook sets itself up around what you actually have going on: a campus job, maybe a tutoring or gig-app side income, a scholarship or grant, and probably your first-ever tax return. No accounting background needed.

### What's different about the student setup

When you pick **Student** during sign-up, AgentBook swaps the usual freelancer categories for ones that actually match your life:

- Tuition & fees, textbooks and course materials
- Part-time job income, tutoring / gig income
- Scholarship / grant income
- Student loan interest, rent, meal plan

You can change this any time under **Configure → Business profile**.

### The value: money you might be leaving on the table

A lot of student tax situations look scary but are actually straightforward once someone explains them in plain English — that's what the agent is for. Ask it things like:

- *"Is my scholarship taxable?"*
- *"I got a $10,000 scholarship, $8,000 to tuition and $2,000 to my dorm — is any of it taxable?"*
- *"Do I qualify for an education credit?"* (US: AOTC / Lifetime Learning Credit) or *"How does the tuition tax credit work?"* (Canada: T2202)
- *"Is my RESP or 529 withdrawal taxable?"*

The agent works from a fixed set of IRS/CRA-grounded rules rather than guessing, and it always tells you when a number depends on the current tax year so you know to double-check it. **AgentBook isn't a CPA or e-file agent** — it drafts guidance and gets your books ready; you (or a free filing partner like a campus tax clinic, or software like FreeTaxUSA/Wealthsimple Tax) do the actual filing.

### Logging your first transaction

Once you're set up, just tell the agent what happened:

- *"log $40 textbooks"*
- *"log campus job paycheck $650"*
- Drop a photo of a receipt or a T4/W-2 and the agent reads it for you

### International students

If you're studying on a visa (F-1/J-1 in the US, a study permit in Canada), onboarding asks one extra question and routes you to guidance built for your situation — nonresident tax status, FICA exemption, Form 8843, and what a 1042-S actually means. AgentBook doesn't try to be your 1040-NR filing engine (that's what Sprintax or GLACIER Tax Prep are for, usually free through your school's international student office) — its job is explaining the parts nobody teaches you and tracking your everyday spending in the meantime. Ask the agent:

- *"I'm an F-1 student, am I a nonresident alien?"*
- *"What does my 1042-S mean?"*

### Student Success add-on — scholarships, careers & housing

Everything above is free. If you want AgentBook to actively help you chase money and sort out where to live, the **Student Success** add-on ($49/year in the US, $65/year in Canada, $59/year in Australia) unlocks three copilots:

- **Scholarship Copilot** — a real, cited search for scholarships that fit *you* (your school, program, and — if you're international — awards you're actually eligible for), plus a tracker that walks you through each application. Every result links to the source; nothing is made up.
- **Career & Co-op Copilot** — the same grounded search for student jobs, internships, and co-op placements near you. If you're on a visa, it only surfaces roles you can legally take (on-campus / CPT / OPT) and flags the work-authorization caveats.
- **Housing Copilot** — save the rentals you're comparing and see each one checked against *your real budget* (pulled from your own income), plus opt-in **roommate matching** with other students.

You don't have to open a plugin page to use any of this — just ask, in chat or Telegram:

- *"Find me scholarships for a computer science major in Ontario."*
- *"Find a co-op near Austin, on-campus only."*
- *"Find me a roommate near campus."*

The agent runs the same grounded search behind the scenes and saves what it finds straight to your tracker — no separate UI required.

Two promises that never change: **AgentBook consults, it never submits.** It helps you find, compare, and prepare — you always do the actual applying yourself on the real site. And roommate matching is **off until you turn it on**, stores no contact details, and only ever shows compatibility — it never messages anyone for you.

Student Success is available in the US, Canada, and Australia.

### Sharing with a parent

If a parent or guardian helps you keep an eye on your finances, you can create a read-only summary link under **Settings → Sharing** — no login needed to view it, and you can revoke or create a new one any time. It only shows a summary, never lets anyone make changes.
