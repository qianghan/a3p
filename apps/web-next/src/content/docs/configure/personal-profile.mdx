## Personal profile

Your **business profile** tells AgentBook how to book your transactions. Your **personal profile** tells your agent who *you* are — so its answers about taxes and personal finance are actually about your situation, not a generic rule of thumb.

### Why it's worth filling in

Ask a tax or money question with no profile, and the agent has to answer in the abstract or ask you to spell things out every time. With a complete profile, it already knows your filing status, dependents, employment type, and rough income — so it can give a specific, useful answer immediately, and it carries that context into every conversation, not just the ones that mention "tax."

![Before and after comparison: a generic answer without a profile versus a personalized, contextual answer once the profile is complete](/docs/personal-profile/before-after-chat-context.svg)

### What it doesn't ask for

Your personal profile never asks for a government tax ID (SSN, SIN, etc.). Everything else useful to an accountant — name, date of birth, address, marital status, dependents, employment type, occupation, and a rough income estimate — but nothing that sensitive.

### Completing your profile

1. If you haven't finished it yet, you'll see a one-line reminder banner on your dashboard. It's dismissible any time — completing it later from Settings works just as well.

   ![Dashboard banner nudging the user to complete their personal profile for better tax and financial advice](/docs/personal-profile/dashboard-reminder-banner.svg)

2. Open **Settings → Personal Profile** (or tap **Complete profile** on the banner). Fill in what you're comfortable sharing — every field is optional, though the more you add, the more specific your agent's advice gets.

   ![Settings, Personal Profile tab, showing fields for name, date of birth, address, marital status, dependents, employment, and estimated income, plus a save button](/docs/personal-profile/settings-personal-profile-form.svg)

3. Select **Save profile**. You can come back and update it any time — life changes, and so should your agent's picture of it.

### Add your past tax returns too

While you're there, the same tab has an **upload past tax returns** option. Adding your last 1-2 years of returns (PDF) gives your agent real history to work from — the same file the agent already checks whenever you ask a tax question in chat. It's optional, but the two together — profile plus past returns — is what turns "here's a general rule" into "here's what to do given the last two years and where you're at now."
