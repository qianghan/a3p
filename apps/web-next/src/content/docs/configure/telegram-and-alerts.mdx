## Telegram & alerts

AgentBook goes where you are. Connect Telegram to capture expenses, ask questions, and get proactive alerts without opening the app.

### Connect Telegram

Telegram bots are self-serve — you create your own bot in about a minute, then hand its token to AgentBook.

1. Go to **Settings → Chatbots** and open the **Telegram** card.
2. Open **@BotFather** in Telegram, send `/newbot`, and follow the prompts to name your bot.

   ![Telegram conversation with BotFather: sending /newbot, naming the bot, and receiving the API token](/docs/telegram/botfather-conversation.svg)

3. Copy the API token BotFather gives you and paste it into the Telegram card, then select **Connect Bot**.

   ![Settings, Chatbots tab, Telegram card not yet connected, showing the three setup steps and a field to paste a bot token](/docs/telegram/settings-chatbots-telegram-not-connected.svg)

4. Open your new bot in Telegram and send `/start`. Your chat is now tied to your AgentBook account — messages there act on your books.

   ![Telegram card after connecting, showing the bot username, active webhook, linked chats, and a disconnect option](/docs/telegram/settings-chatbots-telegram-connected.svg)

From Telegram you can snap a receipt photo, say *"log $12 parking"*, or ask *"what did I spend on software this quarter?"* — the same agent, same data.

> **Tip:** The bot is yours — only messages sent to it reach your books. Disconnect and reconnect anytime from the same card.

### Proactive alerts

The agent watches for things worth flagging and nudges you:

- **Unusual spend** — a charge well above your normal for that category.
- **Missed deductions** — categories where you likely have deductible spend you haven't logged.
- **Reminders** — bills coming due, quarterly tax set-asides, an invoice gone unpaid.

Tune what you receive under **Configure → Notifications**. Web push can be enabled too, once notification keys are set for your workspace.
